**General principle (the thread through #1, #2, #8, #11, #12):** prefer **gates that DERIVE truth from ground truth** (git hashes, file/spec hashes, lane position) over **gates that trust a stored flag or an attestation doc** — and **call the authoritative gate, never reimplement it locally** (#11: a local revalidation of the plan "passed" while the real `pipeline_validate_plan` would have rejected it). Every attestation *and every reimplementation* is an abuse/divergence surface — `patch_state` proved the first, `mapcodebase_fresh` and the Phase 7 local validator prove the second. Where the truth is computable, compute it; where a validator exists, run *that one*.

**Quick wins:** #4 (finalize-round action), #6 (implementation-status grounding), and #8 (derive mapcodebase freshness) are the cheapest. #4 and #6 would have prevented, respectively, the only *legitimate* `patch_state` workaround and the phantom-hole findings that dragged this session; #8 closes the freshness-attestation abuse you just flagged.
