The arc

The pipeline turned its tools on itself. The goal: a validation leg — a 13-subcommand CLI (validation_emission.py) that lets a conductor derive a ConOps from the spec manifest, draft human-judgeable rows, lint them, ship a Telegram digest to Jason (the sole human validator), parse his replies under a strict grammar, manage the judgment lifecycle, self-check against fizzy's close gates, and emit the system-validation artifact.

The spec was debated v1→v5 across 4 rounds with Codex and Gemini, then thrown into the gauntlet where 7 adversaries × 2 models surfaced 669 raw concerns — funnelled to 62 unique themes, 48 accepted. Decomposition produced a 25-card plan (19 components, 6 decomposed parents). Then a multi-agent implementation loop ran overnight: the test suite grew 556 → 760, four incidents were logged with zero data lost, and 16 of 19 components cleared every gate. Three cards remain — two doc sections sitting in Review (Claude authored, so Claude can't review them), and the dogfood close-out blocked on those two.
